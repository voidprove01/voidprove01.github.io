Research

Privacy Choices in Personalized Search Markets

This paper studies consumers’ incentives to share data in exchange for better personalization in a competitive search marketplace. Consumers face a trade-off: enabling cookies improves match quality, but also changes price competition among sellers in ways that may hurt them. I show that a no-privacy regime can improve match efficiency and expand market demand, and that a platform maximizing consumer welfare would prefer to enable cookies while pooling consumers by search cost. When privacy choice is left to consumers, however, a separating equilibrium may arise, leaving nearly all consumers worse off than under the consumer-optimal outcome. The results suggest that consent-based privacy regulation may generate unintended welfare losses.

Selling a New Product to the Crowd: A Mechanism Design Approach

This paper studies a monopolist selling a common-value product to buyers who privately observe independent signals. The seller commits to a mechanism that elicits information from buyers, chooses what to disclose, and sets prices. I show that any mechanism involving private, buyer-specific communication can be replicated—and weakly improved—by a single public disclosure. Under Bayesian incentive compatibility, the optimal mechanism reduces to posted pricing with threshold allocation; a dominant-strategy implementation arises through leave-one-out transfer schemes. I also identify economically relevant settings in which coarse information disclosure becomes strictly optimal despite the absence of capacity constraints.

Consumer Search, Comparison Frictions, and Conversion Timing in Marketplace Browsing (WIP)

This project studies how local choice-set density affects user search and purchase timing in a secondhand marketplace. I combine reduced-form evidence from clickstream behavior—including dwell time, revisit intensity, and within-session convergence toward similar products—with a structural model of sequential search. The model is estimated using simulation-based minimum-distance methods to recover comparison frictions, stopping behavior, and heterogeneity in browsing strategies, allowing me to distinguish strategic waiting from alternative mechanisms such as attention overload or composition effects.

Technical Projects

A selection of technical projects spanning causal inference, simulation-based evaluation, and AI-assisted analytical workflows.

LLM-assisted experiment diagnostics and reporting agent [GitHub]

Built a Python toolkit for automated diagnostics of large-scale online experiments, supporting SRM detection, covariate balance checks, treatment effect estimation, and bootstrap-based uncertainty quantification. Developed an LLM-assisted analysis agent that synthesizes experiment diagnostics, summarizes treatment effects, and generates structured investigation reports to accelerate experiment interpretation and data-driven decision making.

Estimating long-term treatment effects under persistent interventions

Built a simulation framework to compare benchmark estimators for long-term treatment effects in settings with ongoing treatment exposure. Analyzed when surrogate-index methods underperform relative to sequential approaches, and how performance depends on treatment dynamics, proxy quality, and the relationship between short-term and long-term outcomes.

Dynamics of Belief and Relationship Co-evolution on Social Networks

This paper develops an agent-based model to study the co-evolution of opinions and social ties in dynamic networks. Simulations on real-world social networks, including Facebook ego data, show that global consensus is robust to network structure at high tolerance levels, while low tolerance leads to endogenous polarization and social segregation even in well-connected networks.

Applied Industry Experience

Merchant Lifetime Prediction & Uplift Evaluation of GnE Cost @ ByteDance

Developed and deployed a unified ML modeling framework to forecast merchant lifetime and estimate causal impact of experience-related costs, trained on multi-terabyte transaction-level data. Automated daily data ingestion, retraining, and dashboard updates, improving forecast accuracy by about 10% and supplying key inputs to an ROI framework for evaluating long-term merchant outcomes and governance strategies.

Strategy Impact Surface for Platform Governance @ ByteDance

Combined platform-scale A/B experimentation with causal-model insights to build a framework for quantifying the long-term ROI of governance policies. Estimated an upper bound of governance intensity beyond which long-term GMV loss is likely, providing quantitative guardrails for sustainable strategy design.

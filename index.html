About Me

I am a data scientist with a PhD in Economics from Carnegie Mellon University. My work combines theoretical modeling with applied machine learning and causal inference to understand and optimize large-scale platform behaviors. I study how algorithms, incentives, and policy interventions shape outcomes in marketplaces, and I bring this perspective into practical decision-making in industry.

I have hands-on industry experience at ByteDance/TikTok, Uber, and Duolingo, where I worked on experimentation, uplift modeling, ranking and recall systems, and policy evaluation using large-scale log data. My applied work is informed by the theoretical tools I use to think about incentives, screening, and algorithmic design.

Before my PhD, I completed my M.A. in Quantitative Methods (Data Science track) at Columbia University and my B.A. in Economics and Philosophy at Peking University. This background gave me strong foundations in quantitative analysis, formal reasoning, and structured problem solving.

Applied Industry Experience

Merchant Lifetime Forecasting & Governance Uplift Modeling @ ByteDance/TikTok

Developed and deployed a multi-task causal ML system to forecast merchant lifetime and estimate uplift from governance-related experience costs, trained on multi-terabyte transaction-level data. Automated end-to-end daily data ingestion, retraining, and dashboard updates, improving forecast accuracy by about 10% and operationalizing predictions across millions of merchants. The project was selected for presentation at a company-wide e-commerce data science forum.

Strategy Impact Surface for Platform Governance

Combined platform-scale A/B experimentation with causal-model insights to build a framework that quantifies the long-term ROI of governance policies. Mapped nonlinear trade-offs between user experience and seller retention and identified high-leverage governance intensities, informing policy decisions for product and operations teams.

Merchant Risk Modeling

Engineered features capturing operational uncertainty and marketplace behavior to enhance TikTok’s merchant risk models; several features were adopted as production tags. Partnered with cross-functional teams to scope and design new data signals to support governance and enforcement decisions.

Dynamic Pricing via Reinforcement Learning

Selected Research

My research focuses on platform governance, algorithmic screening, and how incentives interact with automated decision systems. Below are a few representative projects.

Algorithms at the Gate

A modeling framework for how platforms set screening thresholds, how commitment or non-commitment shapes seller incentives, and how automated screening trades off quality and variety. Provides intuition for how algorithmic decision systems behave when agents can respond strategically.

Privacy & Search Costs

A theory of how personalization, consumer privacy choices, and price discrimination interact in competitive search markets. Offers insights into how information design and cookie policies affect user experience, prices, and marketplace efficiency.

Manipulable Signals

Models how scoring algorithms respond when sellers can manipulate or game signal distributions, and how commitment or coarsening can mitigate gaming incentives while preserving useful variation in scores.

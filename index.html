About Me

I am a final year PhD candidate in Economics from Carnegie Mellon University, Tepper School of Business. My work combines economic theory with applied machine learning and causal inference to understand and optimize large-scale platform behaviors. I study how algorithms, incentives, and policy interventions shape outcomes in marketplaces, and I bring this perspective into practical decision-making in industry.

Previously, I have interned at ByteDance/TikTok's Platform Governance and Experience team, where I worked on large-scale experimentation, uplift modeling, and policy evaluation using platform log data. My applied work is grounded in the theoretical tools I use to understand incentives, strategic behavior, and information design in marketplaces.

Before my PhD, I received my M.A. in Quantitative Methods (Data Science track) from Columbia University and my B.A. in Philosophy and Economics from Peking University.

Applied Industry Experience

Merchant Lifetime Forecasting & Governance Uplift Modeling @ ByteDance/TikTok

Developed and deployed a multi-task causal ML system to forecast merchant lifetime and estimate uplift from governance-related experience costs, trained on multi-terabyte transaction-level data. Automated end-to-end daily data ingestion, retraining, and dashboard updates, improving forecast accuracy by about 10% and operationalizing predictions across millions of merchants. The project was selected for presentation at a company-wide e-commerce data science forum.

Strategy Impact Surface for Platform Governance @ Bytedance/Tiktok

Combined platform-scale A/B experimentation with causal-model insights to build a framework that quantifies the long-term ROI of governance policies. Mapped nonlinear trade-offs between user experience and seller retention and identified high-leverage governance intensities, informing policy decisions for product and operations teams.

Selected Technical Projects

Hybrid Recall + Ranking for News Recommendation

Built a two-stage recommender system using public news datasets, combining ANN-based candidate retrieval with ML ranking models for 300k users and 360k articles. Demonstrates end-to-end recommender development, including feature engineering, model training, and evaluation.

Dynamic Pricing via Reinforcement Learning

Developed a reinforcement learning environment using NYC For-Hire Vehicle trip data to simulate platform supply–demand dynamics. Trained pricing agents under uncertainty to optimize driver earnings, illustrating the use of RL for economic decision-making and policy optimization.

Privacy Choices in Personalized Search Markets

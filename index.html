Shuoqi Sun

Economics PhD · Platform Governance · Data Science

I am a final-year Economics PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Professor Ali. My research studies platform governance, algorithmic screening, seller selection, and the economics of AI. Methodologically, my work blends information economics, mechanism design, and industrial organization with insights from modern digital platforms.

I am also deeply interested in the intersection of economics and data science. My recent applied work involves experimentation, causal inference, and large-scale modeling at technology companies such as ByteDance/TikTok, Uber, and others. Beyond research, I enjoy building clean machine learning systems, designing governance experiments, and analyzing marketplace dynamics.

Research

• Algorithms at the Gate: A theory of platform governance, algorithmic thresholds, and the quality–variety frontier under commitment and no-commitment.
• Privacy and Search Costs: How consumer privacy choices interact with price discrimination and platform personalization in competitive search markets.
• Screening under Manipulable Signals: A model of algorithmic screening when sellers can manipulate the score distribution through costly effort.

Selected Projects

• Two-stage news recommendation system (recall + ranking) for 300k users and 360k articles.
• Lift-in-experience-cost modeling for e-commerce governance at ByteDance/TikTok.
• Dynamic pricing via reinforcement learning using NYC For-Hire Vehicle trip data.
• User segmentation for Duolingo using K-means on behavioral and survey features.
